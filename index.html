The Rosary High School

Hi!

We are a well-established, Catholic, English Medium educational institution for boys and girls, that has earned a reputation in the holistic development of our students. The school provides the students with a warm and happy atmosphere where everyone can feel secure. We encourage and strive for the highest academic standards, believing that every student should experience success. At the same time, we value each student for his / her individual worth. We have well-qualified and efficient teaching staff that is committed to providing high quality education and help the students to achieve their full potential. We strive to adhere to our motto, “Light the World with Learning”, and pride ourselves in our achievement of academic excellence.

About Us

The Rosary School was founded in 1975 by Late Mr. Vincent Rosario &Mrs.Thecla Rosario in a small room of their niece’s residence in Miramar. On observing the wonderful teaching methods being employed to teach the students, the school grew to its present strength. The secondary school got its recognition in 1986 and sent up its first batch of students for the S.S.C. (Examinations) in March 1987. It wasn’t long before the need was felt to obtain Government aid for the school. The secondary section became a grant- in- aid school from1989. Fatima de Sa was appointed as the first Head Mistress on 05th June 1989. Tony Rodrigues took over as the Chairman of the Rosary Education Society in 2009 At present the secondary section has 700 students. The class size is not more than 42 students. Examination results are an important consideration and we are delighted with the increased levels of success that our students have enjoyed over the last few years. The school attains excellent results at the Goa Board Examination.

We aim to develop our students academically, socially, and morally, so that they leave the institution as independent, cooperative, responsible, and creative young individuals, positively contributing to society. We believe that this can be achieved in the following ways. • By providing opportunities for individuals to develop their abilities to appropriate levels. • By providing effective guidance which will enable individuals to review their progress and to set targets for themselves. • By nurturing self-esteem and encouraging our students to value themselves. • By encouraging respect for other people and an awareness of their needs. • By encouraging achievement and rewarding success. • By providing a varied programme of extracurricular activities.
